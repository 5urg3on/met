Why it matters

Security isn't a
luxury. It never was.

The notion that serious security is only for large organisations is exactly what attackers count on. SMBs are targeted precisely because they're assumed to be unprotected. The technology and the expertise to change that exist. They just need to be deployed properly.

01

The breach has probably already started

The average attacker spends 277 days inside a network before detection. By the time an alert fires, lateral movement, data exfiltration, and persistence mechanisms are often already in place. Detection alone isn't a strategy.

02

Your application is the new perimeter

APIs, web apps, and mobile surfaces are now the front door. Broken authentication, insecure direct object references, mass assignment vulnerabilities. These don't require nation-state capabilities to exploit. They require patience and a browser.

03

A passed audit is not a security posture

Every CISO knows the difference: compliance frameworks measure whether controls exist. Penetration testing measures whether those controls work. The MGM, SolarWinds, and Change Healthcare incidents all happened inside certified organisations.

FAQ

Questions worth
asking.

If I've never had a breach, do I actually need a security assessment?

The absence of a detected breach doesn't mean your systems are clean. It often means attackers are quiet. Most intrusions go undetected for weeks or months. A security assessment finds what's already there and closes the door before that changes.

What's the real difference between a compliance audit and a penetration test?

A compliance audit tells you whether your controls exist on paper. A penetration test tells you whether those controls actually stop an attacker. Compliance gets you certified. Penetration testing tells you if that certification means anything.

We use a cloud provider. Doesn't that mean our infrastructure is already secure?

Cloud providers secure the infrastructure. You're responsible for everything on top of it: your IAM configuration, data encryption, network rules, application logic, and access policies. The shared responsibility model is where most cloud breaches happen.

How early in a product's life should security be brought in?

Before the first line of production code. Security built into the architecture from the start is exponentially easier to maintain than security bolted on after launch. Threat modeling at the design stage is the highest-leverage investment a product team can make.

Our developers are careful. Is a VAPT still necessary?

Careful developers build secure logic. But they're also the ones who wrote the code, which means they carry the same blind spots. An external VAPT tests your application the way an adversary does, without the assumptions your team brings to it.

What happens after the engagement ends?

You receive a full technical report, an executive summary for non-technical stakeholders, and remediation guidance per finding. For VAPT engagements, one post-remediation retest is included. Every deliverable is written to be actionable, not filed and forgotten.
